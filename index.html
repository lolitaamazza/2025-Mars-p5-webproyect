MARS

About the project

My name is Lola Mazza, a second-year Multimedial Technology student. This project grows out of my interest in communicating science through design, using visual language and interaction to make complex content accessible and engaging. I chose Mars because it sits at the intersection of scientific relevance (being the most plausible target for near-future human missions) and a striking aesthetic that supports a clear, immersive visual narrative. My goal this year was to learn p5.js, so the site presents information in cards that open interactive sketches inspired by Martian phenomena (climate, terrain, orbits). This way, users can read, explore, and manipulate generative visualizations, while I bring together code, UX, and visual direction in a cohesive product.

WHY MARS?

At an average distance of 140 million miles, Mars is one of Earth’s closest habitable neighbors. Mars is about half again as far from the Sun as Earth is, so it still has decent sunlight. It is a little cold, but we can warm it up. Its atmosphere is primarily CO₂, with some nitrogen and argon and a few other trace elements, which means that we can grow plants on Mars just by compressing the atmosphere. Gravity on Mars is about 38% that of Earth, so you would be able to lift heavy things and bound around. Furthermore, the day is remarkably close to that of Earth.

Mars vs Earth

Earth is bigger, warmer and wrapped in a thick, oxygen-rich atmosphere. Mars is smaller, colder and has a thin CO₂ atmosphere, but its day length and polar caps make it our most relatable neighbor. Below are the parameters that matter for human missions.

Diameter

Mars 6,779 km · Earth 12,742 km

Mass

Mars 6.42 X 10^23 kg Earth 5.97 X 10^24 kg

Day length

Mars 24h 37m · Earth 23h 56m

Year length

Mars 687 d · Earth 365 d

Atmosphere

Mars CO₂ 95% · Earth N₂ 78%, O₂ 21%

Moons
